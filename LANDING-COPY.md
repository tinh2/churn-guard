## How it works (section copy)

**Step 1: Connect Stripe**
One-click OAuth. ChurnGuard gets read-only access to your subscriptions and registers for cancellation, payment failure, and usage events. Nothing is charged or modified.

**Step 2: Claude flags risk**
As events come in, ChurnGuard sends each customer's data to Claude — MRR, subscription age, payment history, usage signals. Claude returns a risk score and reason in plain English.

**Step 3: Personalized win-back email goes out**
High-risk customers get an email written specifically for their situation. Not a generic template. Claude drafts it, SendGrid delivers it, you get the credit.

**Step 4: Watch your dashboard**
Every save, every recovered dollar, and your ROI vs. the subscription cost. You'll know exactly what ChurnGuard earned you this month.

## FAQ

**Q: Will ChurnGuard make changes to my Stripe account?**
No. ChurnGuard uses read-only OAuth access. It can see your customer and subscription data, but it cannot create charges, modify subscriptions, or touch your billing settings. Ever.

**Q: How does the AI know what to write in the emails?**
We send Claude the customer's MRR, how long they've been a customer, whether a payment failed, and what triggered the alert. Claude uses that context to write an email that references the customer's situation specifically — not a generic "we miss you" blast.

**Q: What if I already use an email tool like Customer.io or Intercom?**
ChurnGuard is focused on the moment of cancellation risk — it's not a general marketing platform. Most customers use it alongside their existing email stack. Win-back emails from ChurnGuard are triggered by Stripe events, not marketing segments.

**Q: How fast do win-back emails go out?**
Within seconds of the Stripe webhook firing. When a customer hits "cancel," ChurnGuard receives the event, Claude scores the risk, and the email is sent — typically in under 30 seconds total.

**Q: What happens if a customer complains about the email?**
Every email includes an unsubscribe link. If a customer replies upset, the email came from your From address — so they're contacting you, which is actually valuable signal. ChurnGuard logs all sends, opens, and clicks so you have full visibility.
